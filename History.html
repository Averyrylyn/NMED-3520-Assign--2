A Brief History Of Cats

Cats are some of the most popular pets in the world, they can be found anywhere from homes to television and overwhelmingly on the internet. It’s a wonder why they became so popular to begin with. It seems like cats have always been around, however the cats we are familiar with today have been naturally domesticated to have the qualities and appearances they do now.

While it is assumed that our feline friends originally descended from the African wild cat given, they share similar qualities. A burial site in Cyprus was the first archaeological find that indicated that cats lived alongside humans, as far as 9,500 years ago.

The domesticated cat can be traced back to Egypt 4,000 years ago. They were most likely useful in Egypt because they helped reduce disease and protected crops from pests. Cats were quickly regarded as sacred creatures in Egypt. When Egypt became part of The Roman empire cats became widespread across Europe around the 4th century AD. From Europe it’s thought that cats made their way over to America by boarding ships. By the end of the 19th century cats were starting to be kept as company in America. And by World war I cats were commonly accepted as pets in the US. Over thousands of years, a new species of cat eventually evolved and naturally made their homes around people.
