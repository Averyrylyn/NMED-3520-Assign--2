What Is Required?

While cats are generally low maintenance compared to dogs the responsibility of owning a cat should not be taken lightly. Cats can typically live between 12-18 years which means they are a long commitment, there are several different factors that should be considered when adopting a cat. Factors such as do they have a suitable environment to live in? What expectations do I have about owning a cat? Do I have the time commitment?

In addition, cats will also require a lot of necessities, aside from just the basic needs cats will need to be provided with a fair amount of human companionship, a stimulating and safe environment. They will need their vaccinations, to be groomed, a scratching post and a clean and comfortable bed. It is also important to understand that all cats are different and how each cat will interact with you is inherent on personality and early life. The environment in which you live will also affect a cat greatly, what other pets do you have? Do you live with children or infants? Answering some of these questions will better help you become more understanding of your expectations about owning a cat.

Cats also come in many shapes and sizes, depending on the breed of the cat some will be long-haired, short-haired, striped, and spotted too. There is a large variety of cat breed that comes with their own sets of personality, skills, and physical differences.
